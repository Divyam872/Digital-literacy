Stay Safe Online – Cybercrime Prevention Checklist
Never share your OTP, UPI PIN, passwords, or bank details with anyone, even if they claim to be from the bank or customer support.
Do not click on unknown or suspicious payment links received through messages, emails, or social media platforms.
Always verify the UPI ID and the name of the receiver before making any payment through digital payment applications.
Remember that scanning a QR code is used to pay money, not to receive money. Never scan a QR code sent by an unknown person.
Avoid responding to fake job or internship offers that ask for registration fees or payment through UPI.
Use strong and unique passwords for your email, social media, and banking applications, and change them regularly.
Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on important accounts to add an extra layer of security.
Do not share personal information such as your phone number, address, college ID, or bank details on social media.
If you become a victim of cybercrime, immediately report it on the National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in).
You can also contact the Cybercrime Helpline Number 1930 for quick assistance in case of online financial fraud.
